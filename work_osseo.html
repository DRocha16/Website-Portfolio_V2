002 : Forearm Prothesis w/ Osseointegration

Why osseointegration? Because it's a way to improve the feedback that the amputee has from the prothesis movement. Every touch on the prothesis will produce vibrations in it that will be carried to the bone, where this can be naturally sensed by the body.

"Patients with an osseointegrated limb have better physical control over – and a more intimate, emotional connection to – their prosthetic leg or arm, compared to those using a traditional socket prosthesis."

HSS Hospital

Overview

This was a project being made for a course of my masters. This first phase, I had to develop an implant to fix the prothesis to the bone (osseointegration).

The implant mode of operation was based on a porous fixture that is permatently implanted into the bone, it is porous to let the bone grow through it, integrating with it. Then, an abutment will be screwed to the fixture. On the other hand, the abutment will fit into a 2 lock mechanism conector. The first mechanism is meant for a quick first fit (it is partly inspired in the water hoses quick fit mechanism), then for added safety there's a screw lock mechanism.
